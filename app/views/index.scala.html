About Outdoor Preschool

Outdoor Preschool is a Vermont-licensed early childhood education center.
Classes are offered five days a week, from 8:30AM to 1:00PM and 8:30AM to 3:30PM.

The School

Children thrive outdoors, which is why the Outdoor Preschool program is based in nature. Children are supported by compassionate and skilled educators who enjoy being outside, caring for and learning from children’s unique curiosity and perspectives.

The Outdoor Preschool program was established in Vermont in June of 2015 by Annabelle Knight. The idea began in California in 2012 while Annabelle was working for Google Inc. As an educator at one of their childrens’ centers, she examined how nature walks impacted children’s and adult’s experience of the natural world and enjoyment of learning. Her interest led her to seek an internship with the owner and lead teacher of Leaping Lizards, an entirely outdoor preschool in Northern California. After a few weeks working alongside Magdalena Cabrera, Annabelle began to search for a location to found a new nature-based school on the east coast.

Curriculum

Our program offers children a dynamic and engaging, culturally responsive environment that supports the whole child (physical, emotional, social and cognitive development and well-being). The program integrates many foundational skills: critical thinking, problem-solving, collaboration, cooperation, social and environmental responsibility.

Each day of our program is guided by children’s play, interactions with their environment and daily routines. We provide children with ample free time for unstructured play, which is a child’s primary mode of learning. Educators encourage experiential, hands-on learning, which allows children’s play, imagination and imitation to guide everyday work. The physical environment is an unlimited source of rhythm, meaningful patterns and daily inspiration. We regularly incorporate the use of photos, videos, fine arts materials, music as well as natural materials found on our grounds. Throughout the year, children contribute their work to a portfolio as evidence of their progress in the program. The portfolio is used during conferences to reference a child’s work and is always accessible for families to see.

For a more detailed explanation of school activities and target developmental skills, please view the Skill Development Guide.

Mission & Philosophy

Mission Statement

The mission of Outdoor Preschool is to provide children with time to explore, appreciate and connect with their environment while building a variety of essential life skills. Outdoor Preschool strives to cultivate an inclusive community of lifelong learners and ecologically conscious citizens through nurturing strong, respectful partnerships among adults, children and the natural world. We value and advocate for families’ voices, diversity, unique perspectives and expert knowledge of their children. Outdoor Preschool does not discriminate on the basis of gender, race, color, religious beliefs, national and ethnic origin, age, socioeconomic status, marital status, family structure, disability, or sexual orientation.

Philosophy

The Outdoor Preschool program combines play-based and child-led philosophies while focusing on the outdoor environment as the primary learning space. Educators act as facilitators of children’s learning, providing stimulation and support only as needed. We nurture children’s innate curiosity, creativity and healthy risk-taking in the outdoors, which enables them to connect and grow independently, and as community members. Outdoor Preschool aims to promote equity, while actively challenging prejudices and injustices through anti-bias education. Through meaningful interactions in nature, children learn how to take care of themselves, and how to respect themselves, each other, and the Earth.

Our program emphasizes social and environmental responsibility with the goal of providing contextually and developmentally appropriate educational care. We acknowledge, encourage and respect that each child (and their family) brings diversity to our community. Educators routinely reflect on their own cultural and social identities, attitudes, strengths and challenges while embracing and facilitating expression of similarities and differences in the community. The program is flexibly structured to balance the needs of individual children, along with the needs of the group.



Why Outdoor Preschool?

Outdoor Preschool is part of a growing movement across communities worldwide for more nature-based early learning programs. Outdoor learning contributes to a more positive and sustainable future for our planet as it provides greater understanding of and connection to the natural world we live in.

Play

Outdoor Preschool emphasizes a child’s right to play which is a child’s work. We plan for at least 3 hours of child-led, unstructured play time each day. Although children’s play might appear simple and repetitive, they often demonstrate tremendous mastery of complex concepts. For example, a 4-year-old pretending to fly a plane by sitting on top of a fallen tree is using her imagination to construct symbolic meaning, a complicated cognitive task. She might ask peers to join her and act as passengers, which reveals her developing ability to collaborate with others. Researchers have documented significant impacts of play in terms of school readiness and healthy development of the brain, including social, emotional and physical skills4,6. Ultimately, play enables children to direct their own creative learning processes and guide their own development.

Physical Activity

Our program incorporates a high amount of physical activity, especially because we are outside much more often than traditional preschool programs. Research shows that nearly half of preschool children do not get enough active play, also referred to as moderate to vigorous physical activity (MVPA)9. Current governmental guidelines recommend that children ages 3-5 years old engage in at least 60 minutes of MVPA/active play each day. We provide children with 3+ hours of outdoor play each day. The outdoor environment is more likely to encourage active play (MVPA) compared to indoor environments5. We designed our program with the knowledge that children are healthier in the long-term, more able to learn and better able to self-regulate when their entire bodies are actively engaged in their work1,7.

Engaging with Nature

The natural world presents a huge variety of learning opportunities, health and wellness benefits. Experts agree that time spent outside encourages positive social, cognitive, emotional and physical development3. When it comes to the best environments for children’s learning, the natural world promotes the ideal balance of healthy risks, challenges and fun. Children and adults experience restorative cognitive functioning, improved motor skills and lower stress levels when interacting with or looking at nature compared to non-natural, urban environments2,10,11. Additionally, some children have shown to have enhanced ability to concentrate when spending time near natural environments8.

  1. Initial Contact: Interested family contacts Outdoor Preschool

Commonly Asked Questions

  • Do you always go outside?
    Yes! If weather conditions drop below 15 degrees or above 90 degrees Fahrenheit, there is heavy precipitation, thunder, lightning or high winds, children’s time outdoors will be limited. Outdoor Preschool has many options for indoor activities. Educators follow exposure recommendations from the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Association (NOAA) website for wind chill and frostbite risk.
  • Are there requirements for this program?
    Yes. All children must have turned 3½ years of age by the time they are enrolled and attending classes. Additionally, children must be toilet-trained and properly dressed for the outdoors. Please read ‘Required Clothing and Gear’ in the Family Handbook for further information.
  • What is your teacher to child ratio?
    4.5:1 There are always two educators working with a maximum of 9 children each day.
  • What qualifications do the teachers have?
    The lead educator, Annabelle Knight, has a Master’s Degree in Early Childhood Education from Lesley University. She is a licensed and certified Master Teacher and Director in the state of Vermont. To learn more about staff backgrounds, please see Staff or Testimonials.
  • Are snacks and lunch provided?
    Yes and No. Lunch is not provided. Snacks are provided. We always offer organic, vegan snacks in the morning and afternoon in order to reduce our carbon footprint and nourish our bodies. We recommend zero-waste lunches that use reusable packaging and promote healthy eating habits. If children forget their lunch at home, the following items will be available:
    • Fresh, organic fruits and vegetables, such as apples, pears, carrots and peppers
    • Organic, vegan crackers and whole grain bread
    • Organic hummus and black bean dip
  • How far do you travel?
    When children are newly enrolled, our adventure hikes will consist of about ½ -1½ miles total distance walking. As children grow stronger and more familiar with the routine, we can walk up to 3 miles in a morning.
  • What is anti-bias education and why is it important?
    Research has shown that children as young as 3-4 years old have developed biases around differences such as skin color and ability. At Outdoor Preschool we want to promote equity and justice by actively combating bias and prejudice. The anti-bias education approach supports children to identify and talk about differences and similarities. We emphasize feeling comfortable and proud of unique attributes and common humanity.
  • Anti-bias Education Goals
    • Each child will demonstrate self-awareness, confidence, family pride, and positive social identities.
    • Each child will express comfort and joy with human diversity; accurate language for human differences; and deep, caring human connections.
    • Each child will increasingly recognize unfairness, have language to describe unfairness, and understand that unfairness hurts.
    • Each child will demonstrate empowerment and the skills to act, with others or alone, against prejudices and/or discriminatory actions.
    Derman-Sparks & Edwards, Anti-bias Education for Young Children and Ourselves 2010, p.xiv
  • What does family participation look like?
    Families are always welcome and encouraged to visit the school. Outdoor Preschool loves to have visitors help out and teach us new things whether it is for 5 minutes, 45 minutes or a day. We ask that families who are visiting, dress appropriately for the weather, including wearing similar items that we ask children to wear in order to model appropriate gear. When participating in Outdoor Preschool, we ask families to adhere to the following policies:
    • Adults do not help children climb higher than the child can climb by him or herself.
    • We can pick up material that is no longer living and we avoid picking up items that are still alive.
    • Children may take home pieces that are smaller than a finger and inanimate.
    • We can use sticks for walking if the child can adequately control their motion and sticks may be incorporated into play if they are responsibly moved.
    • Smoking is prohibited on all school property.
